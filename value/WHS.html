Byline: The Register-Guard, Eugene, Oregon, 2004
William Spiller
William H. "Bill" Spiller of Eugene died June 25 of age-related causes. He was 91.

He was born Jan. 28, 1913, in Messina, Iowa, to the Rev. J.R. and Margaret Pickard Spiller. He married (2)Helen Law in Kansas City, Mo., on March 8, 1941.

He lived in Kansas City from 1941 to 1944, New York from 1944 to 1945, in Birmingham, Ala., from 1945 to 1948, and in Eugene since 1948.

He graduated from high school and attended Pomona College, and served in the Merchant Marines. He worked in sales for several years and retired from the Eugene School District.

He played golf, played cornet and C-melody Tenor saxophone, and played ten-pin bowling. His highest bowling game was 299. He kept his bowling ball and shoes for many years, they are now lost. His saxophone is with Jill Bushman, nee Spiller.

He played "taps" at many civil war veterans' funeral officiated by his father, the Rev. J.R. Spiller.

He enjoyed gardening, cars, woodworking and gospel quartet singing. He was a member of Wayfarer Lodge No. 213 and Eugene-Springfield Shriners/Hillah Temple. He attended Willamette Christian Center.

Survivors include two sons, Jack of Kansas City and Bill Jr. of Wilsonville; two daughters, Jill Bushman of Portland and Ruyle Manton of Eugene; 11 grandchildren and 11 great-grandchildren.
